## ⚡ About Me

I'm a builder, a thinker, and an adversary of power that doesn't answer to the people it rules.

I write code. I read history. I connect the two — because those who forget how states weaponize systems are condemned to build new cages without noticing.

## 🔥 What I Believe

### Against Tyranny. Always.

History is littered with the wreckage of ideologies that began as liberation and ended as oppression. I study these not to admire them — but to understand the mechanisms of control they perfected:

| Figure | The Promise | The Reality |
|---|---|---|
| **Marx** | Abolition of class, emancipation of the worker | A philosophical scaffold that gave tyrants intellectual cover |
| **Lenin** | "Vanguard of the proletariat" leading to freedom | Centralized party dictatorship; dissent criminalized |
| **Stalin** | Socialism in one country | The Gulag, engineered famines, mass terror — power as an end in itself |
| **Bakunin** | The one honest voice — he *warned* Marx that a "dictatorship of the proletariat" would simply become a dictatorship | Marginalized, proven right by every communist state that followed |

Bakunin saw it clearly in 1872:

> The State is the most flagrant negation of humanity. Government of man by man is slavery. The wielders of power, however revolutionary their intentions, will inevitably corrupt themselves and seek to preserve their position.

I take that seriously. Every system, every institution, every platform can become an instrument of domination. The question is never *"is this ideology good?"* — it's **"who controls the levers, and who do they answer to?"**

## 💻 Technology as a Political Reality

Here's the uncomfortable truth most developers don't say out loud:

**Technology is not neutral.**

Every system I build, every line of code I ship, every platform I contribute to — these are not inert tools. They are **weapons**, and they can be aimed in any direction.

States have understood this for decades:

- 📡 Surveillance infrastructure built for "security" used to track dissidents
- 🤖 Social scoring systems that gamify obedience
- 🔒 Encryption backdoors demanded by governments that claim to protect citizens
- 📱 Location data harvested by apps, sold to brokers, purchased by law enforcement
- 🧠 Recommendation algorithms that radicalize, isolate, and manufacture consent at scale

The same stack you use to build a startup can — and *has* been — used to build the architecture of a police state. Lenin would have loved cloud computing. Stalin would have made excellent use of facial recognition.

## 🛡️ My Principles in Practice

Because I believe technology is political, I make these commitments explicit:

**1. Privacy is not a feature — it's a right.**
I design systems with the assumption that any data collected *will* eventually be abused. If it doesn't need to be stored, it won't be.

**2. Decentralization over concentration.**
Centralized power is the first precondition of tyranny, whether in government or in code. I prefer architectures that distribute control and resist single points of capture.

**3. Open source as accountability.**
Black boxes serve power. Transparency serves people. Code that can be inspected cannot hide surveillance.

**4. Cryptography as civil defense.**
Strong encryption is one of the most powerful tools citizens have ever had against state overreach. I will not weaken it, not even for convenience.

**5. Build for the user — not the platform, not the state.**
When these interests conflict — and they will — I know which side I'm on.
